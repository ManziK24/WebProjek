Snake Plant

Description

Often called Mother-in-Law's Tongue, the Snake Plant is the undisputed champion of the "un-killable" plant world. With its stiff, sword-like leaves shooting upward in architectural clusters, it is the perfect blend of modern minimalism and rugged endurance.

Whether you are a self-proclaimed "black thumb" or a seasoned collector, the Snake Plant is a staple.

Care Instructions

Light

The Snake Plant is incredibly versatile. While it prefers bright, indirect light, it is famous for its ability to thrive in low-light corners where other plants would struggle.

Watering

The number one way to kill a Snake Plant is by being too kind with the watering can. These plants store water in their thick leaves and can go weeks without a drink.

Soil and Drainage

Because root rot is its only real enemy, use a free-draining cactus or succulent mix. A terracotta pot is an excellent choice for a Snake Plant.

Temperature and Humidity

Snake Plants are not picky. They are comfortable in standard household temperatures and don't require any extra humidity.
