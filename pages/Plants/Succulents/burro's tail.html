Burro's Tail

Description

The Burro's Tail (or Donkey's Tail) is a show-stopping succulent famous for its long, cascading stems packed with fleshy, tear-drop shaped leaves. Its soft, blue-green hue and "braided" appearance make it the ultimate choice for hanging baskets or high shelves where it can trail elegantly toward the floor.

Native to southern Mexico, this plant is as hardy as it is beautiful, adding a whimsical, sculptural texture to any indoor plant collection.

Care Instructions

Light

Burro's Tail thrives in bright, indirect sunlight. It loves a good soaking of morning sun but can get "sunburned" if left in intense, direct afternoon heat.

Watering

Like most succulents, the Burro’s Tail stores water in its leaves. It is very drought-tolerant but sensitive to overwatering.

Soil and Drainage

Well-draining soil is non-negotiable. Use a cactus or succulent potting mix amended with perlite or coarse sand.

Temperature and Humidity

This plant prefers average room temperatures (18°C – 24°C). It is not frost-hardy, so if you keep it outside during summer, bring it indoors before the first frost.
